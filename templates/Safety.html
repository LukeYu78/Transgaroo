Safety Tips

Cycling Equipment

Before riding, bicycle riders are required to have certain equipment to keep safe. All bikes must have at least one working brakes, a bell or horn, and lights. Cyclists must wearing a suitable helmet when riding.

Learn more »

Traffic Signs

Bicycle riders must obey traffic control signs and signals including traffic lights, stop and give way signs. Cyclists must give way to any right-side vehicle on the crossroads when there are no lights or signs.

Learn more »

Hand Signals

Give a clear indication of where you are going and signal the direction you intend to travel. Bicycle riders must give hand signals when turning right including changing lanes and making a U-turn.

Learn more »

Follow Bicycle Lanes

Bicycle riders must ride on bicycle lanes in the same direction as vehicles. Keep to the left on shared pathways, watch out for car doors opening and be part of the traffic stream. Don't weave in and out of traffic.

Learn more »

Speed Limitation

Bicycle riders must obey the speed limit. The speed limit in built-up areas of the city without signs is 50 km/h, 40 in schools and shopping areas, and 10 in areas marked by a ‘Shared Zone’ sign shared with pedestrians.

Learn more »

Parking Rules

Bicycle riders can park the bike on a footpath as long as it’s not in the way or breaking any local laws, cannot park in where with ‘No Parking’ sign. Parking in a public area should use a bike rack or rail.

Learn more »
